
Project Title: HR Employee Turnover Dashboard

1. Project Goal:

I built an interactive Excel dashboard to help HR managers understand exactly why employees are leaving the company. By exploring a dataset of nearly 1,500 employees, this tool makes it easy for management to click, filter, and spot turnover trends across different departments and job roles so they can make smarter decisions about retaining their team.

2. How I Built It

. Data Prep & Cleaning:** I started by organizing the raw data into dynamic Excel Tables. I used `IFS` formulas to group complex numbers (like age and salary) into simple categories (like "Entry Level" or "Senior Level") to make reporting easier.

. Data Analysis: I used PivotTables as the "engine" to calculate the core metrics the business cares about, such as total headcount, the overall turnover percentage, and average salaries.

. Visual Design:** I designed a clean, modern dashboard interface using custom KPI cards and simple, effective charts (Donut, Column, and Horizontal Bar charts) without any messy gridlines or clutter.

. Interactive Features:** I added Slicers (clickable filters) and connected them to the entire dashboard. This allows any user to instantly filter the charts and numbers by Department, Education, or Gender with a single click.

3. Key Business Insights

. High Turnover in Sales:** Sales Representatives are leaving at a rate of nearly 40%, which is massively higher than the company average of 16%. The company needs to focus heavily on retention strategies specifically for the Sales team.

. The Work-Life Balance "Sweet Spot":** Employees who rate their work-life balance as terrible (Level 1) leave the fastest (31%). Surprisingly, the most loyal employees aren't at Level 4—they are at Level 3, with only a 14% turnover rate. Aiming for a "good" balance is actually more effective for retention than forcing a "perfect" one.

. Pay Matters:** The numbers clearly prove that employees who quit had a significantly lower average monthly salary compared to the employees who decided to stay.
